Current Script
If you decide to sign up and use the current scripts menu what will appear will be all of your saved scripts along with scripts that you have bought on the store and scripts that have been shared with you via script share if you haven’t saved bought or shared anything then you will get a blank screen. If you have saved bought or shared scripts then a menu you come up that looks something like this.
The menu items will appear in chronological order with the most current items at the top of the list. As you can see there are 2 different menu styles! The ones that have been bought and the ones that are saved! Scripts that have been shared can either be bought or saved scripts. With the saved and the bought scripts, the table has been divided into 2 sections. The first section will show the scripts heading and a description if you save a script it is important that you give it a heading but description is an optional item and only really necessary if your wanting to upload and sell your script. A description will help sell your script to a potential consumer and if they can see something in the description that they can identify with they will be more willing to have a look at it.
If you can look at the saved scripts in the second section of the table there is category, sub category date saved and how many templates on the script. The category is the category of what Industry the script of benefit for? If we use the example script for instance then you would say it’s for the recruiting industry which would be broadly classified into human services. The sub category is what type of sales is it for e.g. outbound, inbound, business development etc. How many templates is handy so you can gauge how big your script is and the date saved is the date that you last edited it. In the top right-hand corner, you can see you have the option to upload the script or Edit the script.
By clicking the editing function, it enables you to go into the script templates themselves and from their you can change your script to whatever you want or need it to be. The heading, description, Category and sub category can all be changed via the save as option or save option when editing the script.
If you decide to upload your script for sale this screen should appear. This is so you can upload your script on the Sales Script Market where buyers have the option to purchase your script for an allocated price. Note this is why its handy to have a good description and to choose a category and sub category plus allocate a realistic price. The market will determine how much your script is worth so if you’re not selling many scripts then you may need to adjust the price or if your selling scripts by the hundreds then you might even see fit to raise your price for the script.
You will notice with the bought scripts on the second section of the table their will be some additional information such as how many users the script has the star rating and the date it was uploaded. Every user when purchasing a script has the option to rate the script after they have used it which gets averaged out into a star rating out of 5 stars. If you can see how long a script has been on the market for compared to how many users it has it will give you a good indication of how popular a script is and its value in the market. Bought scripts can’t be uploaded again nor can they be edited.

Your current scripts give you autonomy on what scripts to use and when best to use them and I would highly recommend having a good collection of scripts at your disposal a different type of clients may need to have different scripts used on them even if its in the same industry.
